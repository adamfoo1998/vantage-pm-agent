# Head-of-Product Brief (plain-language pass)

Goal: take a correct-but-technical output and make it land with a non-specialist, Taiwan-based
audience without losing precision. This is usually a *second pass* over another skill's output.

## Transform rules
1. **BLUF first** — the decision/ask in the first two sentences. Karlos should get the point
   without reading further.
2. **Decode jargon** — replace or gloss CFD/derivatives/MT5 terms. Expand every acronym on first
   use (e.g. "stop-out (the level at which positions are force-closed)"). Keep a short glossary
   at the end only if >5 terms remain.
3. **Tables over paragraphs** for any comparison, option set, or decision matrix.
4. **Make the decision explicit** — if you're asking for a decision, present the options, the
   recommendation, and the trade-offs as a table. Don't make the reader infer the ask.
5. **Length discipline** — one screen for a status/decision brief. If longer, lead with a summary
   box and push detail to an appendix.
6. **Copy-paste clean** — no stray formatting, no unexplained internal codes.

## Preserve, don't dilute
- Keep confidence tiers (`Confirmed`/`Inferred`/`Speculative`) and `VERIFY` flags visible —
  plain language must not erase uncertainty signalling.
- Don't soften a real risk to make the brief read nicer.

## Pre-send checklist
- [ ] First two sentences carry the whole point.
- [ ] No unexpanded acronym; no undecoded jargon.
- [ ] The ask/decision is explicit.
- [ ] Uncertainty flags survived the rewrite.
- [ ] Fits the audience's reading time.
